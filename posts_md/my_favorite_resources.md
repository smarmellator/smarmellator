## Say everything
A few months ago I finished a book called "Say everything" by Scott Rosenberg which detailed the history of blogging with the rise of the internet. It was a very enjoyable read, and it made me realize how much our society is really just become a collection of links.  
Our human knowledge is disseminated in the links that point to the right resource to the right person. On-demand knowledge is just that: the right link at the right moment. The content at the link can be more or less complex, more or less detailed, more or less approachable, more or less serious, but regardless it showcases that the links are where humans have decided to park their wisdom.  
A system of knowledge based on links has multiple advantages:  
1. It's easily accessible by anyone.  
2. It's infinitely composable. Links can link to links ad infinitum, and they can be in constant flux updating each other as needed.  
3. It is hard to conceal good information (unless you forbid entire internet access).  
However, a system of knowledge based on links also has multiple disadvantages:
1. Little gems can get lost in the laws of SEO and other new internet bullshit.  
2. It makes it harder to trust sources, which slows the learning process.
